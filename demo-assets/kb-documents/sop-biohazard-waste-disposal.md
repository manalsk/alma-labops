# SOP: Biohazard Waste Disposal

## Purpose
This SOP explains how members of the Cancer Biology Lab should handle, segregate, label, and dispose of biohazardous waste.

## Applies To
- Cell culture waste
- Contaminated pipette tips
- Gloves contaminated with biological material
- Culture plates and tubes
- Waste exposed to human or animal-derived biological samples

## Procedure
1. Place solid biohazard waste into a red biohazard bag inside a labeled biohazard bin.
2. Do not overfill the bag beyond two-thirds capacity.
3. Close the bag using a secure knot or approved closure.
4. Label the bag with lab name, date, and responsible researcher.
5. Liquid biological waste must be treated with freshly prepared 10% bleach for at least 30 minutes before disposal, unless otherwise instructed by institutional biosafety policy.
6. Sharps must never be placed in regular biohazard bags. Use approved sharps containers only.
7. If a spill occurs, notify the senior researcher immediately and follow the spill response SOP.

## Important Notes
- Never mix chemical waste with biohazard waste.
- Do not leave biohazard bags open overnight.
- Students may assist with waste handling only after onboarding and safety briefing.
- Final disposal pickup must follow institutional biosafety procedures.

## Responsible Role
Senior researcher or PI-approved lab safety lead.
